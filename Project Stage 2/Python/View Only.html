Analysis on cryptocurrency with Pandas¶

Cryptocurrencies have always been one of the greatest fascinations of many tech geeks, speculators and so called investors themselves. This notebook aims to encourage that speculation and that adrenaline rush with cryptocurrency data from the 2014-2016 era in order to show that no one in hindsight could have predicted such a rise that we have now been seeing today in the year 2017.

This analysis is primarily focused on displaying the graphs of Litecoin and Bitcoin during 2014-2016, their mean, median, max, correlation between the two values, and many other analysis of data that is useful from a financial point of view.

# Groups specific columns of the data by the weighted_price 
# Finds the sum of the BTC volume
# This allows us to know where BTC trades occurred the most.

columns = dataframe.loc[:,['vol_aud', 'weighted_price']]
columns = (columns / 100)
columns = columns.round(0) * 100
btc_occurrences = columns.groupby(['weighted_price']).sum()

# Point of control: aka most voluminous areas of the BTC prices
fig, ax = plt.subplots()
ax.plot(btc_occurrences)
ax.set_facecolor('white')
ax.ticklabel_format(useOffset=False, style='plain')
plt.xlabel('Weighted Price of BTC', fontsize=25, fontname='DejaVu Sans')
plt.ylabel('Volume in AUD', fontsize=25, fontname='DejaVu Sans')
plt.title('Total AUD volume at different BTC prices between 2014-2016', fontsize=30, fontname='DejaVu Sans')
plt.setp(plt.gca().get_xticklabels(), rotation=45, horizontalalignment='right', fontsize=18)
plt.setp(plt.gca().get_yticklabels(), fontsize=18)
btc_occurrences.plot(style='b', ax=ax, kind = 'bar', figsize=(18,10))
